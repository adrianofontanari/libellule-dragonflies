## The Grain of Sand

Vulnerable is the grain  
that the wind carries away.

the webbed feet of the seagull avoid it.  
Lapped by many,

Unique is the grain  
that the hand cannot break.  
Wings it does not need  
to see the horizon.  
Yet noisy

in the silence of solitude.  
And it resists.

## I Stopped

I stopped fighting  
and got off the horse.  
There is no way to prevent my being pierced  
by this lance of time.  
Me the fool  
riding the mountains in the stormy night,  
through thunder and hail that grazed me  
while the little birds disappeared.  
Me the dreamer  
who hoped to find you.  
the Sea  
I found only other mountains, some desert.  
And it doesn't matter  
but it hurts  
that in the distance one seems to see some beach  
and believe perhaps to have you near.  
I stop  
here on a nameless mountain,  
among the sun, the pines and the nightingale,  
who undeterred sings  
and cares nothing of my search.  
I caress Desire  
who lives in the present  
living soul  
that wants to keep running  
toward that island that perhaps is not there.
